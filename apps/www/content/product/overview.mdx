# Overview

`opendetail` is a grounded assistant layer for apps that already have useful Markdown and MDX content. It turns that content into answers that stay tied to your own source material instead of drifting into general chat.

## What opendetail is

At its core, `opendetail` is a thin product layer on top of the OpenAI Responses API. You point it at content you already maintain, build a local index, and let your app answer questions from that material. The goal is simple: make your product easier to understand without moving your content into a separate platform or asking a team to rebuild what they already wrote.

The current package stays intentionally small. It ships the runtime, setup-focused CLI commands, and thin framework wiring helpers. That makes it a good fit when you want grounded answers inside your product, but you do not want to stand up extra retrieval plumbing or build a custom Responses integration from scratch.

## Where it fits

`opendetail` fits best when the hard part is not "how do we make a chatbot," but "how do we ship a grounded answer flow with less setup." It works well for product explainers, help content, onboarding material, internal guides, and developer docs that already live in Markdown and MDX.

It is especially useful when a team wants a narrow assistant that can answer common product questions, cite its sources, and fail plainly when the answer is not in the content. That shape is often more useful than a broader assistant that sounds confident but cannot show where an answer came from.

## Who it is for

`opendetail` is for teams that:

- already have product knowledge in Markdown and MDX
- want grounded answers inside an app, route, or hosted transport
- care more about clarity and citations than open-ended chat
- prefer a local index and a small integration surface

It can serve technical and non-technical content alike, as long as the source material is clear enough to answer real questions. The product is only as useful as the content behind it, which is why clear headings, concrete language, and maintained source files matter so much here.

## What it is not

`opendetail` is not a full assistant platform or a general-purpose agent framework. It does not try to replace the OpenAI Responses API. It packages the setup, grounding, and integration ergonomics around that API. It also does not replace your source content. It depends on it.

That boundary is part of the product. `opendetail` is useful because it stays close to the words you chose, the routes you own, and the questions your product actually needs to answer.

## Integration modes

| Mode | What you manage | Best for | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `self-hosted` | Route, runtime env, `OPENAI_API_KEY` | Private docs and server-owned control | Available today |
| `hosted` | Index build and client transport config | Faster setup and future OpenDetail-managed delivery | Documented and scaffolded in this phase |
