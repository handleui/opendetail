# Overview

`opendetail` is a grounded assistant for apps that already have useful Markdown and MDX content. It turns that content into answers that stay tied to your own source material instead of drifting into general chat.

## What opendetail is

At its core, `opendetail` is the engine behind a product knowledge experience. You point it at content you already maintain, build a local index, and let your app answer questions from that material. The goal is simple: make your product easier to understand without moving your content into a separate platform or asking a team to rebuild what they already wrote.

The current package stays intentionally small. It ships the runtime, setup-focused CLI commands, and thin framework wiring helpers. That makes it a good fit when you want grounded answers inside your product, but you do not want to stand up extra search infrastructure or adopt a full assistant platform just to answer common questions well.

## Where it fits

`opendetail` fits best when the hard part is not "how do we make a chatbot," but "how do we keep answers close to what we already wrote." It works well for product explainers, help content, onboarding material, internal guides, and developer docs that already live in Markdown and MDX.

It is especially useful when a team wants a narrow assistant that can answer common product questions, cite its sources, and fail plainly when the answer is not in the content. That shape is often more useful than a broader assistant that sounds confident but cannot show where an answer came from.

## Who it is for

`opendetail` is for teams that:

- already have product knowledge in Markdown and MDX
- want grounded answers inside an app or route
- care more about clarity and citations than open-ended chat
- prefer a local index and a small server-only surface

It can serve technical and non-technical content alike, as long as the source material is clear enough to answer real questions. The product is only as useful as the content behind it, which is why clear headings, concrete language, and maintained source files matter so much here.

## What it is not

`opendetail` is not a hosted knowledge base, a UI kit, or a general-purpose assistant that answers from anywhere. It does not ship a chat interface, authoring workflow, or broad agent behavior. It does not try to replace your source content. It depends on it.

That boundary is part of the product. `opendetail` is useful because it stays close to the words you chose, the routes you own, and the questions your product actually needs to answer.
